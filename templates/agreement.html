Sky Eye User Agreement

Please read the following carefully.


By creating a Sky Eye profile you are signing up to receive text notifications at a designated phone number informing you of severe thunderstorm, tornado, and/or flash flood warnings issued by a Warning Forecast Office (WFO) of the National Weather Service (NWS) that are issued for a designated location. With this the user understands that such warnings are not issued by Sky Eye, therefore Sky Eye can not be held responsible for warning performance, including false alarm warnings and/or missed events. Likewise, the user understands that Sky Eye remains in alpha mode, which means that systems and methods for disseminating NWS warning notifications are still being actively developed and debugged, meaning that Sky Eye alerts may or may not perform with 100% reliability in all situations. It is the user's responsibility to have multiple ways to receive NWS warnings in order to take appropriate action to remain safe. Therefore, Sky Eye can not be held responsible for any bodily harm, property damage, or any additional consequence resulting from a severe thunderstorm, tornado, or flash flood. Furthermore, Sky Eye is not responsible for any consequence that may arise from receiving or not receiving a text notification of a severe weather alert.

Sky Eye takes user privacy seriously and will not share user location or phone number information. While Sky Eye is built with security in mind, in the event of a data breach Sky Eye will notify its users or such breach and give them the option of altering their user information or discontinuing the service.
